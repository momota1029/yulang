# `act copy` and companion visibility

`act copy = source` declares a new effect family whose operation signatures and
companion declarations are inherited from another `act`. It is a copy, not a
type alias: operations emitted through the destination family are distinct from
operations emitted through the source family.

```yu
act base:
    our tick: () -> never

act local = base
```

`local::tick` is an operation of `local`, not of `base`.

## Inherited members

When the compiler materializes a copied act, it reads the source body as an
export surface:

- `pub` and `our` operation signatures are copied.
- `pub` and `our` companion members are copied into the destination companion.
- `my` declarations in the source body are not copied.
- The destination `with` body is a direct body of the destination act, so its
  own `my` declarations remain visible to its own members.

```yu
act source:
    my hidden = 1
    our visible = 2

act copy = source with:
    my local = 3
    our own = local
```

The destination companion contains `visible`, `local`, and `own`. It does not
contain `hidden`, and the `with` body cannot refer to `hidden`.

This is a visibility rule, not an implementation shortcut. Copying an act whose
exported members depend on source-private declarations may therefore be
ill-formed:

```yu
act source:
    my hidden = 1
    our visible = hidden

act copy = source
```

The source act itself is valid. The copied act is not guaranteed to be valid,
because `hidden` is not part of the source export surface.

## Type parameter aliases

Parameterized copies substitute the source act parameters through the
destination parameters:

```yu
act source 'a:
    our get: () -> 'a

act copy 't = source 't
```

The inherited signature is rebased onto `copy`, with source type variables
rewritten through the copy declaration's aliases.

## `with` body ordering

The inherited source export surface is registered before the destination
`with` body. This lets the `with` body override or extend inherited definitions
using the ordinary declaration-order rules of companion modules. Source ranges
for inherited declarations remain attached to the source act, not to the copied
destination member.
